# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

**Student:** Yash Verma | 25MIP10137 | CSE, VIT Bhopal
**Course:** CSE0001 – Digital Literacy

---

## ✅ DO's

1. **Do keep your profiles professional.** Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and a real name on LinkedIn and GitHub. Employers and internship coordinators will search for you.

2. **Do use privacy settings.** Regularly review and adjust the privacy settings on Instagram, Facebook, and other platforms. Control who can see your personal posts.

3. **Do think before you post.** Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or future employer saw this?" If not, do not post it.

4. **Do give credit when sharing others' work.** If you share an article, image, or idea that belongs to someone else, always tag the original creator or cite the source.

5. **Do report harassment and cyberbullying.** Use the reporting tools on every platform. Block and report accounts that send abusive or threatening messages. You can also report to cybercrime.gov.in or call 1930.

6. **Do verify information before sharing.** Fake news spreads fast on social media. Always check the source before sharing news, especially during sensitive events.

7. **Do log out from shared devices.** Never stay logged in to social media or email on a computer lab, library, or friend's device.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Don't share personal or financial details online.** Never post your phone number, Aadhaar number, bank account details, UPI PIN, or home address on any social media platform.

2. **Don't click on unverified links.** Links sent via DMs, comments, or unknown emails may lead to phishing sites designed to steal your information.

3. **Don't engage in or ignore cyberbullying.** Never participate in trolling, hate speech, or online harassment — even if others are doing it. Silence can also be harmful; report what you see.

4. **Don't post academic work that isn't yours.** Plagiarism and sharing copyrighted content without permission can have serious academic and legal consequences.

5. **Don't accept friend or follow requests from strangers without checking.** Fake profiles are commonly used for scams, romance fraud, and social engineering attacks.
