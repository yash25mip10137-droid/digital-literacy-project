**Student:** Yash Verma | 25MIP10137 | CSE, VIT Bhopal

## Tool Used
Canva (canva.com) – free browser-based design tool

## What the Infographic Shows

The infographic is titled "Be Digitally Smart!" and covers three key topics:

1. **What is Digital Literacy?**
   - The ability to find, use, evaluate, and create information using digital tools
   - Essential skill for every student in the 21st century

2. **Safe Internet Practices**
   - Use strong, unique passwords for every account
   - Never share OTPs or personal details online
   - Verify links before clicking — phishing is real
   - Use only trusted Wi-Fi networks

3. **Professional Online Presence**
   - Keep your LinkedIn and GitHub profiles updated
   - Use your college email for academic sign-ups
   - Think before you post — your digital footprint lasts forever

## Design Notes
- Color scheme: Blue and white for a clean, professional look
- Icons used from Canva's free library to represent each section
- Single A4 landscape page, readable at a glance

## Reflection (150–200 words)

For this task, I used Canva, a free browser-based design tool that requires no installation. I chose it because it offers ready-made infographic templates that are easy to customize. My infographic is titled "Be Digitally Smart!" and covers three major topics: what digital literacy means, safe internet practices, and how to build a professional online presence.

The design uses a blue and white color scheme with icons to make the content visually engaging and easy to understand. I included key points such as using strong passwords, avoiding phishing links, and keeping academic profiles up to date on platforms like LinkedIn and GitHub.

One thing I found both difficult and interesting was deciding what to include and what to leave out. There is so much to say about digital literacy, but an infographic must be concise. I had to prioritize the most important points for a first-year student audience. This exercise taught me that effective communication is as much about what you leave out as what you include. It also made me more aware of my own online habits and where I can improve.
