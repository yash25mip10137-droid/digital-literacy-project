# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist

**Student:** Yash Verma | 25MIP10137 | CSE, VIT Bhopal
**Course:** CSE0001 – Digital Literacy

A checklist of actionable online safety tips for college students in India.

---

## 🛡️ General Online Safety

1. **Use strong, unique passwords for every account.**
   Combine uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Use a password manager if needed. Never reuse passwords across platforms.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all important accounts.**
   This includes your email, LinkedIn, GitHub, and banking apps. Even if your password is stolen, 2FA adds a critical second layer of protection.

3. **Never click on links in unsolicited emails or messages.**
   Always verify the sender's email address carefully. Official institutions use domain-specific emails (e.g., @vitbhopal.ac.in), not Gmail or Yahoo.

4. **Check the URL before entering any personal information online.**
   Look for `https://` and verify the domain spelling. Phishing sites often use URLs like `vitbhopall.in` or `sbi-bank.com` to deceive you.

5. **Never share OTPs with anyone — not even someone claiming to be from your bank or college.**
   No legitimate organization ever asks for an OTP over call, SMS, or email.

6. **Keep your devices and apps updated.**
   Software updates include security patches. Outdated apps are easier for attackers to exploit.

---

## 💳 Financial & UPI Safety (Mandatory)

7. **Never share your UPI PIN, CVV, or bank account number with anyone online.**
   Your bank or payment app will NEVER ask for this information. Receiving money via UPI does NOT require entering your PIN — if someone asks you to enter a PIN to "receive" money, it is a scam.

8. **Verify the recipient's UPI ID or QR code before making any payment.**
   Scammers often send fake QR codes that redirect payments to their accounts. Always double-check before scanning.

---

## 📱 Social Media & Identity Safety

9. **Set your social media profiles to private and review your followers/friends list regularly.**
   Avoid accepting requests from unknown accounts. Fake profiles are used for scams, data collection, and harassment.

10. **Do not post photos of your Aadhaar card, PAN card, college ID, or any government document online.**
    Even in a "private" group or chat, shared documents can be misused for identity theft.

---

## 🚨 Reporting – Know Where to Go

If you are a victim of any cybercrime or online fraud:

- 🌐 **National Cyber Crime Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) — File a complaint online, 24x7
- 📞 **Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930** — Call immediately after any financial fraud to increase chances of recovery
- 🏫 **Report to your institution** — Inform your college's IT department if you receive phishing emails targeting students
