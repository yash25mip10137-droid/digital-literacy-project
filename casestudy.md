## Case Study: The Fake Scholarship Email

**Cybercrime Type:** Phishing

### What is Phishing?
Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which an attacker impersonates a trusted organization — such as a bank, university, or government body — to trick a victim into revealing sensitive personal or financial information.

### How It Happened – Step by Step

1. **The Setup:** Rohan, a first-year engineering student, received an email that appeared to be from "VIT Scholarship Department." The email had the college logo and looked completely official.

2. **The Hook:** The email stated that Rohan had been selected for a merit scholarship worth ₹50,000. To claim it, he needed to verify his identity by clicking a link and entering his bank account details and UPI ID.

3. **The Fake Website:** The link led to a website that looked almost identical to the official college portal, but the URL was subtly different (e.g., `vitbhopall.in` instead of `vitbhopal.ac.in`).

4. **The Trap:** Rohan entered his UPI ID, registered mobile number, and the OTP he received (after being told it was for "verification"). Within minutes, ₹12,000 was debited from his account.

5. **The Aftermath:** Rohan realized the fraud only after checking his bank notifications. By then, the attacker had already transferred the money.

### Who is Targeted?
First-year college students are a common target because they are new to digital systems, excited about scholarships, and may not yet recognize the warning signs of phishing.

### Consequences
- Financial loss (₹12,000 in this scenario)
- Compromise of personal banking credentials
- Psychological stress and loss of trust in digital systems
- Risk of identity theft if Aadhaar or other ID details were shared
